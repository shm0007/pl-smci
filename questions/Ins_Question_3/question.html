Scenario: The new Internet company, Blorp, often has sensitive customer data. When sending data over the Internet or storing it on disk, Blorp encrypts all customer data using a widely used encryption algorithm. The customer's identification number is used as the encryption key. Customer Identification Numbers are 32 digits long.

Question: What is the most critical weakness in this system?

 Identification numbers are not secret. They are using a widely used encryption algorithm. A computer could be used to repeatedly guess the encryption key until it finds the right one. Storing the data on the disk is not secure.
