As part of NSF grant #1821788, we (Dr. Peterson and students in the Laboratory for Advanced Research in Systems at UMD) are creating a test to measure students' understanding of basic computer security concepts. As part of this development, we want to know what students believe about these concepts, or what their instinct or common sense tells them is the right answer. This activity is a survey asking for your answers to multiple-choice security questions. You are only graded on participation. It is important that you NOT use any books, friends, or online resources to answer these questions -- we want to know what you know without any outside information, and you will not be graded on the correctness of your answers in any way. If you don't know the answer, make up something that sounds plausible to you. Please treat the content of this survey as confidential and do not share it online.

Investigator: Dr. Peter A. H. Peterson -- pahp@d.umn.edu

Eligibility: Participants must be university students who are at least 18 years old.

Overall Description of Participation:

This activity is an online 35-question multiple-choice survey about computer security concepts. We estimate that it will take you approximately 30 minutes. Taking this test is required for this course, but whether you consent to us using your answers anonymously for research is up to you. You can skip any question you cannot or do not wish to answer.

What will this data be used for?

We will use your responses ANONYMOUSLY to help create/improve a multiple-choice test about the security topics covered.

Risks and Benefits of Participation:

No unusual risks or discomfort are expected from participating.

Confidentiality and Data Use Statement:

We will treat your responses as anonymous and confidential. We will collect your email address to provide extra credit (if applicable). Once your participation information is provided to your instructor, we will remove your identifiers from this data. Apart from this single-use, we will NEVER use your identity with your answers. This will not negatively affect your grades in any way, and nothing about the questions is personal or sensitive in nature.

Institutional Review Board Statement:

This study (STUDY00016809) has been determined to be "not human research" by the University of Minnesota's IRB, because the responses are not both personal and identifiable. If you have questions about this determination or this study, please contact Dr. Peterson at pahp@d.umn.edu or the UMN IRB at irb@umn.edu.
